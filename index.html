Can a small model learn
conservation of energy?

We trained a tiny neural network on a frictionless spring mass system. No friction, no damping, no physics equations given. It just saw position and velocity over time and had to predict what comes next. The curious question was whether something like energy conservation would quietly show up on its own, or whether the model would drift into physically impossible states. Adjust the parameters, hit run, and see what happens.

What to look for

When k is set to 1.0 (what the model trained on), it tracks the true physics closely and energy stays roughly flat on both lines. Try dragging k above 2.0. The model never saw a stiffer spring, so it starts making physically impossible predictions and the energy lines diverge.

This reveals something specific: the model learned the statistical patterns of one spring, not the underlying law. It conserves energy for conditions it recognises, and breaks down everywhere else.
